## 1. Overview

**Median Home Price:** $2,444,677 (per NeighborhoodScout — top 0.7% nationally)
**ZIP Code:** 33301
**Type:** Urban luxury waterfront neighborhood
**Character:** Old Florida charm meets modern luxury. Tree-lined streets, deep-water canals, walkable to Las Olas Boulevard.

Rio Vista is one of Fort Lauderdale's oldest and most prestigious neighborhoods. Developed in the 1920s-1940s, it combines historic Mediterranean Revival and mid-century homes with modern new construction. The "Isles" portion — finger islands with direct waterfront canal access — commands significant premiums.

**Key Demographics (NeighborhoodScout):**
- Top 2.9% wealthiest neighborhoods in the U.S.
- 40.6% of adults hold advanced degrees (top 2.9% nationally)
- 96.1% concentration of married couples (vs. national average)
- 30.4% work from home
- Average rental: $3,034/month

## 2. Boundaries

- **North:** New River / Las Olas corridor
- **South:** SE 15th Street / Davie Blvd area
- **East:** Intracoastal Waterway (east of Rio Vista Blvd)
- **West:** Federal Highway (US-1) / Andrews Avenue

### Rio Vista vs Rio Vista Isles
- **Rio Vista (mainland):** West of the main canal system. Tree-lined residential streets, generally non-waterfront or canal-front. More historic homes.
- **Rio Vista Isles:** The finger islands east of Rio Vista Blvd with deep-water canal access. Higher price points, newer construction, direct ocean access via Intracoastal.

## 5. Pricing Patterns

### Waterfront Premium
Based on Whitaker sales data:
- **Waterfront new construction:** $1,000-$1,500/sqft
- **Waterfront renovated:** $600-$900/sqft
- **Non-waterfront:** $400-$600/sqft
- **Waterfront premium:** Roughly 2-3x non-waterfront

### Price Tiers
- **$10M+:** Ponce De Leon, ultra-premium new construction
- **$5M-$10M:** Prime waterfront new builds (SE 7th, 8th, 10th)
- **$3M-$5M:** N Rio Vista Blvd waterfront, Ponce De Leon renovated
- **$2M-$3M:** Isles waterfront (older homes), S Rio Vista Blvd
- **$1M-$2M:** Interior streets, SE 5th Court, smaller waterfront, SE 9th-11th Avenues
- **Under $1M:** Rare. Non-waterfront fixer-uppers only.

### New Construction vs Original
- New construction trades at roughly 2-3x the price of original 1950s-60s homes on the same street
- Active teardown market: buyers purchasing $1-2M originals, demolishing, building $5-10M homes
- This is driving the neighborhood's price escalation

## 8. Neighborhood Comparisons

### vs Las Olas Isles
- **Location:** Directly north of Rio Vista, along Las Olas Blvd
- **Price range:** Generally higher ($3M-$25M+)
- **Vibe:** More "see and be seen," Las Olas party proximity
- **Key difference:** Las Olas Isles = prestige address; Rio Vista = more family-oriented, slightly more privacy

### vs Harbor Beach
- **Location:** South of Rio Vista, beachside
- **Price range:** $5M-$30M+
- **Vibe:** Gated oceanfront community, ultimate privacy
- **Key difference:** Harbor Beach has beach access and a private beach club. Rio Vista is more neighborhood/community feel.

### vs Victoria Park
- **Location:** North of Rio Vista, north of Las Olas
- **Price range:** $500K-$2M
- **Vibe:** More bohemian, walkable, restaurants
- **Key difference:** More affordable entry point but lacks waterfront/canal access

### vs Colee Hammock
- **Location:** Between Las Olas and Broward Blvd
- **Price range:** $800K-$3M
- **Vibe:** Historic, tree-canopied, eclectic architecture
- **Key difference:** Smaller lots, more urban feel, no deep-water access

### vs Idlewyld
- **Location:** East of Rio Vista, between Intracoastal and beach
- **Price range:** $3M-$15M+
- **Vibe:** Intimate island community, very private
- **Key difference:** Closer to beach, smaller neighborhood, fewer homes available

## 9. Investment Outlook

### Bull Case
- Continued migration from NY/NJ/CA to South Florida
- Limited inventory — very few buildable lots remain
- New construction replacing 1960s homes is lifting comps for entire neighborhood
- Proximity to downtown Fort Lauderdale development (Brightline, new hotels)
- Climate/lifestyle appeal

### Bear Case
- Insurance costs rising across South Florida
- Flood risk concerns with sea level projections
- Interest rates affecting luxury market
- Property tax increases on new construction assessments

### Trend
- Rio Vista is in a multi-year price acceleration driven by teardown/rebuild cycle
- Streets that were $1M in 2018 are now $3M+
- Expect continued appreciation as remaining original homes get replaced
